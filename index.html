Earth

Did you know that the Sun accounts for a staggering 99.86% of the total mass of our entire solar system? That means all the planets—including massive Jupiter—moons, asteroids, and comets combined make up less than 0.2% of the system's "stuff." Because of this immense mass, the Sun has a powerful gravitational pull that keeps everything from Mercury to the distant Oort Cloud in orbit. To visualize this scale, if the Sun were a hollow ball, you could fit roughly 1.3 million Earths inside it. It isn't just a star; it is truly the heavy anchor of our cosmic neighborhood.
